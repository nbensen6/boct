Pre-Game Setup

Physical Setup

  • Arrange chairs in a rough circle/square with a definite clockwise order
  • Leave a gap between two chairs so you can enter/exit easily
  • Keep the center clear of tables or tripping hazards
  • Position your Grimoire where players walking around won't accidentally see it

Character Selection (Trouble Brewing)

  • Use the setup sheet to determine how many Townsfolk, Outsiders, Minions, and Demon based on player count
  • A solid beginner-friendly 8-player setup: Washerwoman, Librarian, Investigator, Chef, Empath, Fortune Teller, Undertaker, Drunk (register as Investigator), Scarlet Woman, Imp
  • Put selected character tokens in the bag, have players draw one each secretly

First Night Prep

  • Have your night sheet ready with the night order
  • Pre-plan what information you'll give to drunk/poisoned players
  • Place reminder tokens you'll need on the left side of the Grimoire

Rules Explanation

Keep it simple! Tell players only four things they need to remember:

  1. You may say whatever you want at any time - talk publicly or privately
  2. No peeking - keep your token secret, never look in the Grimoire
  3. Ask me questions anytime - even evil players can ask how their bluff character works
  4. Dead players still matter - you can talk, you win/lose with your team, and you get one final vote

Storyteller Tips: Night Phase

  • Move around the circle randomly so footsteps don't reveal who you're waking
  • Tap shoulders/knees quietly to wake players
  • When evil players bluff characters that manipulate the Grimoire, pretend to move tokens so veterans can't tell the difference
  • Use consistent signals: 2 taps = eyes open, finger to lips = eyes closed

Balancing the Game

Your goal is to get the game to the final 3-player showdown - that's when it's most exciting. Help the weaker team:

If Good is Crushing Evil:

  • Give the drunk Empath correct info
  • Let the Spy register as a Townsfolk

If Evil is Dominating:

  • Give poisoned players accurate information
  • Have the Mayor's night attack kill a Minion instead

Handling Misinformation

  • You're allowed to lie to drunk/poisoned players - they won't know their info is bad
  • Plan what false info you'll give BEFORE night starts so you don't scramble
Don't Break the Rules - Even if it seems fun, stick to the rules. You have plenty of flexibility within the rules to create drama.

Narration Tips

You can narrate as much or as little as you want. Here are some ideas:

It was a dark and stormy night in Ravenswood Bluff. On the stroke of midnight, a scream echoes through the town square...

Creative Deaths

Before executions, ask: "How would you like to die?"

Let players be creative - walk off a cliff, death by chocolate, struck by lightning, etc.

Announcing Victory

  • Don't just mumble "evil wins"
  • Build dramatic pause, get everyone's attention
  • Announce with flair so the winning team can celebrate properly!
Not comfortable narrating? That's fine too. A mostly silent Storyteller still runs great games - the players create their own fun.

Common New Storyteller Mistakes

  • Rushing nights - Slow down and double-check your work
  • Forgetting reminder tokens - Track poison, drunk status, and used abilities
  • Nudging player choices - Let players make weird decisions; they might have a plan you don't see
  • Over-explaining to new players - Drip-feed rules as needed rather than dumping everything upfront
  • Post-game "you should have done X" - Avoid shaming, it kills the vibe

Beginner-Friendly 13-Player Setups

With 13 players, you have 9 Townsfolk, 0 Outsiders, 3 Minions, and 1 Demon. This is a significant jump - you're at maximum minions, which makes the game considerably more challenging.

Setup 1: Information-Heavy (Recommended for First Game)

Townsfolk (9):

Washerwoman Librarian Investigator Chef Empath Fortune Teller Undertaker Ravenkeeper Slayer

Minions (3):

Poisoner Scarlet Woman Baron*

Demon (1):

Imp

*Baron adds +2 Outsiders, so swap 2 Townsfolk for Saint & Drunk

Why it works: Tons of information characters get players talking immediately. The Baron adds Outsiders which creates noise and bluff opportunities for evil. New evil players have safe bluffs available.

Setup 2: Balanced with Protection

Townsfolk (9):

Washerwoman Librarian Chef Empath Fortune Teller Undertaker Monk Soldier Mayor

Minions (3):

Poisoner Spy Scarlet Woman

Demon (1):

Imp
Why it works: Monk and Soldier give good some protection against 3 minions poisoning/killing. The Spy helps evil coordinate. Mayor gives an alternate win condition.

Setup 3: Classic with Training Wheels

Townsfolk (9):

Washerwoman Librarian Investigator Chef Empath Fortune Teller Undertaker Virgin Slayer

Minions (3):

Poisoner Spy Baron*

Demon (1):

Imp

*Baron adds +2 Outsiders, so swap 2 Townsfolk for Drunk + Saint

Why it works: Virgin can confirm players early. Slayer gives good a direct shot at the demon. Baron creates outsider confusion.

Tips for Running 13 Players

  • 3 minions is a lot - Evil has significant poisoning/information corruption power. Consider helping good subtly if they're struggling
  • Give evil 3 good bluffs - On first night, show the Demon 3 characters not in play
  • Minions know each other - All 3 minions wake and learn who the other minions and demon are
  • Games run longer - Budget 60-90 minutes
  • More dead players = more dead votes - Remind everyone that dead players still win/lose with their team and get one final vote

Have Fun!

The wiki at wiki.bloodontheclocktower.com is a great reference if you hit any weird edge cases mid-game.

Good luck, Storyteller. The fate of Ravenswood Bluff rests in your hands...
